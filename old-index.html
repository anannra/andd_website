Strategic Deployment

Opportunity Zone Aligned

ANDD targets federally designated Opportunity Zones — placing infrastructure where it creates the most impact and unlocking powerful tax incentives for investors.

What Are Opportunity Zones?

Established by the 2017 Tax Cuts and Jobs Act and made permanent by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act in 2025, Opportunity Zones are 8,764 designated census tracts across all 50 states and U.S. territories where new investment qualifies for preferential tax treatment.

Over $100 billion in private capital has been deployed into OZs through approximately 7,800 Qualified Opportunity Funds — and about 25% of datacenter square footage proposed or under construction is already in designated zones.

Why OZs Are Ideal for ANDD

  • Underserved communities that lack digital infrastructure and connectivity
  • Sub-2MW edge facilities deliver economic benefits without straining local power and water
  • Creates construction and permanent skilled operations jobs in distressed areas
  • Drives local workforce development and technology training programs
  • Generates tax revenue supporting schools, roads, and public services
  • Bridges the digital divide by placing compute closer to end users

Investor Tax Incentives

Capital Gains Deferral

Defer tax on eligible capital gains by reinvesting into a Qualified Opportunity Fund with a rolling 5-year deferral window.

Basis Step-Up

Receive a 10% step-up in basis at 5 years — or up to 30% for rural OZ investments through Qualified Rural Opportunity Funds.

10-Year Exclusion

Hold for 10+ years and pay zero tax on all appreciation of the new investment. Datacenter infrastructure naturally aligns with this long-term hold period.
